<li><b>LC_Stratum </b></li></ul>
Individual or groups of land cover elements may be grouped into strata (or layers). The LC_Stratum metaobject represents a grouping of land cover elements, LC_Element, that make a layer. This layer can then be considered as the definition of a component of a LC_LandCover metaobject. Several stratum may be combined to model specific land cover classes in certain environments. An example is a savannah or woodland composed by three separate layers of trees, shrubs and herbs with different cover of the woody component of the vegetation types. The cover of the elements in each stratum may overlap since each stratum is independent from each other. Examples of the use of the LC_Stratum and the layering construct is given in Annex C.<br/>LC_Stratum metaobject may be instantiated to form a component of a land cover class in a land cover classifications system, and form a component of a legend class in a legend derived from that classification system.<br/>This metaobject has three relationships:<br/>It is an element in the composition relationship <i>multiLayerComposition</i> with the LC_HorizontalPattern metaobject.<br/>It has a composition relationship <i>stratumComposition</i> with the metaclass LC_Element.  A constrain on this relationship is that {the sum of the cover of all the LC_Elements within one strata can not be more than 100%}.<br/>There is an optional <i>verticalRelationship</i> relationship between separate instances of land cover class components instantiated from the LC_Stratum metaobject. The properties of this relation are given in the association class LC_InterStrataRelationship.<br/>The constraint on this relationship is that an LC_Stratum metaobject may have a <i>verticalRelationship</i> only with the next metaobject in the ordered list of LC_Strata that belong to the same LC_LandCover metaobject.<br/><br/>
