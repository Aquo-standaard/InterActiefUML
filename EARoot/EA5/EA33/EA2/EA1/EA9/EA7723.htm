This example describes a boreal and hemi-boreal forest system where the tree layer is in two different heights. The trees are in two parts, the overstore trees, the understory trees, and a separate shrub layer and forbs layer. The example shows two layers of natural trees. In this example there is intentionally no height, cover or Leaf type/leaf phenology attribution in order to remain very general. A third layer of shrubs and a fourth layer of forbs are described. An addition element describing the climatic area could have been added but is not included in the example for simplicity.  <br/>The model  shows a class EL_BorealAndHemiborealForest composed of elements that correspond to instances of the metaclasses from LCML. The class EL_BorealAndHemiborealForest has been built up with four vegetation layers consisting of two layers of trees,a layer of shrubs and a layer of forbs. This example has been kept very simple and no attributes have been used to characterize the trees, shrubs or forbs.<br/><br/>
